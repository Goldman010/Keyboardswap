Listing Lifecycle

Listings move through the following statuses:

Draft (future)
Pending Review
Approved
Scheduled
Live
Sold
Unsold
Removed

Every listing must be approved by an administrator before becoming visible to the public.

Administrators determine the auction start date and time.

Once approved, sellers can view the scheduled auction start time.

When the auction begins, the listing automatically transitions to Live.

When an auction ends successfully, the listing becomes Sold.

If an auction ends without meeting the reserve price, it becomes Unsold.

Sold and Unsold listings remain publicly viewable for historical pricing and research and can be filtered in search results.

Administrator Review Checklist

Every submitted listing should be reviewed before approval.

Administrators should verify:

Required photos are present and clearly show the item.
Photos accurately represent the listed item.
Listing title is descriptive and accurate.
Description is complete and truthful.
Category is correct.
Category-specific specifications are complete.
Starting bid appears reasonable.
Reserve price (if present) appears reasonable.
Duplicate listings do not already exist.
Images are not stolen or obviously misleading.
Counterfeit or prohibited items are rejected.
Listing complies with marketplace rules.
Auction is scheduled for an appropriate date and time.

Administrators may:

Request additional photos.
Request clarification from the seller.
Reject listings that do not meet marketplace standards.
Feature exceptional listings on the homepage.

The goal is to ensure every public listing maintains a high standard of quality and trust.

Marketplace Rules
Auctions

Default auction duration is 7 days.

Auctions end at the scheduled time unless extended by anti-sniping.

Once the first bid is placed, the bid increment cannot be changed.

Proxy Bidding

Users may set one maximum proxy bid per auction.

The system automatically bids only enough to maintain the user's leading position.

Proxy bid maximums are private and never visible to other users or sellers.

Anti-Sniping

If a bid is placed within the final 30 seconds of an auction, the auction end time is extended by 2 minutes.

Additional qualifying bids continue extending the auction until no bids are placed during the final 30 seconds.

When less than 2 minutes remain, listings display a highlighted Ending Soon badge.

If the auction enters overtime, the badge changes to Extended.

Reserve Price

Reserve prices remain hidden from buyers.

Listings display one of:

Reserve Not Met
Reserve Met
No Reserve

If the reserve is not met after the auction ends, the seller may privately contact any bidder to negotiate a final sale price.

Questions & Answers

Any logged-in user may ask public questions before the auction ends.

Questions and answers are publicly visible.

Only the seller may answer listing-specific questions.

Administrators may remove inappropriate content.

Questions remain part of the listing history.

Ratings

Each completed transaction creates one opportunity for each party to leave a rating.

Users may rate the same person multiple times across different transactions.

Ratings are permanently associated with the completed transaction and are never deleted.

Transaction States
Completed
Canceled by Buyer
Canceled by Seller
Refunded
Disputed
Resolved
Rating Eligibility

Ratings are only available after a transaction reaches Completed or Resolved.

Mutual cancellations before fulfillment do not allow ratings.

If one party is determined to be at fault during a refund or dispute, only the non-fault party may leave a rating.

Administrators may override rating eligibility when resolving disputes.
